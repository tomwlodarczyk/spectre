34 /*!
35  * \brief Newtonian isentropic vortex in Cartesian coordinates
36  *
37  * The analytic solution to the 2-D Newtonian Euler system
38  * representing the slow advection of an incompressible, isentropic
39  * vortex \cite Yee1999. The initial condition is the superposition of a
40  * mean uniform flow with a gaussian-profile vortex. When embedded in
41  * 3-D space, the isentropic vortex is still a solution to the corresponding 3-D
42  * system if the velocity along the third axis is a constant. In Cartesian
43  * coordinates \f$(x, y, z)\f$, and using dimensionless units, the primitive
44  * quantities at a given time \f$t\f$ are then
45  *
46  * \f{align*}
47  * \rho &= \left[1 - \dfrac{(\gamma - 1)\beta^2}{8\gamma\pi^2}\exp\left(
48  * 1 - r^2\right)\right]^{1/(\gamma - 1)}, \\
49  * v_x &= U - \dfrac{\beta\tilde y}{2\pi}\exp\left(\dfrac{1 - r^2}{2}\right),\\
50  * v_y &= V + \dfrac{\beta\tilde x}{2\pi}\exp\left(\dfrac{1 - r^2}{2}\right),\\
51  * v_z &= W,\\
52  * \epsilon &= \frac{\rho^{\gamma - 1}}{\gamma - 1},
53  * \f}
54  *
55  * with
56  *
57  * \f{align*}
58  * r^2 &= {\tilde x}^2 + {\tilde y}^2,\\
59  * \tilde x &= x - X_0 - U t,\\
60  * \tilde y &= y - Y_0 - V t,
61  * \f}
62  *
63  * where \f$(X_0, Y_0)\f$ is the position of the vortex on the \f$(x, y)\f$
64  * plane at \f$t = 0\f$, \f$(U, V, W)\f$ are the components of the mean flow
65  * velocity, \f$\beta\f$ is the vortex strength, and \f$\gamma\f$ is the
66  * adiabatic index. The pressure \f$p\f$ is then obtained from the dimensionless
67  * polytropic relation
68  *
69  * \f{align*}
70  * p = \rho^\gamma.
71  * \f}
72  *
73  * On the other hand, if the velocity along the \f$z-\f$axis is not a constant
74  * but a function of the \f$z\f$ coordinate, the resulting modified isentropic
75  * vortex is still a solution to the Newtonian Euler system, but with source
76  * terms that are proportional to \f$dv_z/dz\f$. (See
77  * NewtonianEuler::Sources::IsentropicVortexSource.) For testing purposes,
78  * we choose to write the velocity as a uniform field plus a periodic
79  * perturbation,
80  *
81  * \f{align*}
82  * v_z(z) = W + \epsilon \sin{z},
83  * \f}
84  *
85  * where \f$\epsilon\f$ is the amplitude of the perturbation. The resulting
86  * source for the Newtonian Euler system will then be proportional to
87  * \f$\epsilon \cos{z}\f$.
88  */
