47 /*!
48  * Compute the 2nd moment of the neutrino distribution function
49  * in the inertial frame (pressure tensor) from the 0th (energy density)
50  * and 1st (momentum density) moments. The M1 closure sets
51  * \f{align}{
52  * P_{ij} = d_{\rm thin} P_{ij,\rm thin} + d_{\rm thick} P_{ij,\rm thick}
53  * \f}
54  * with \f$P_{ij}\f$ the pressure tensor in the inertial frame, and
55  * \f$P_{ij,thin/thick}\f$ its value assuming an optically thick / optically
56  * thin medium.
57  *
58  * Following the algorithm described in Appendix A of \cite Foucart2015vpa,
59  * we choose the Minerbo closure (\cite Minerbo1978) which sets
60  * \f{align}{
61  * d_{\rm thin} = & 1.5\chi-0.5\\
62  * d_{\rm thick} = & 1.5-1.5\chi \\
63  * \chi = & \frac{1}{3} + \xi^2 \frac{6-2\xi+6\xi^2}{15} \\
64  * \xi = & H^aH_a/J^2
65  * \f}
66  * with \f$J\f$ the energy density in the
67  * frame comoving with the fluid, and \f$H^a\f$ the momentum density
68  * in that frame.
69  * The optically thick closure is
70  * \f{align}{
71  * K^{ab,\rm thick} = \frac{J_{\rm thick}}{3} (g^{ab}+u^a u^b)
72  * \f}
73  * with \f$ K^{ab,\rm thick} \f$ the pressure tensor in the frame comoving with
74  * the fluid, and \f$ u^a \f$ the fluid 4-velocity, while the optically thin
75  * closure is
76  * \f{align}{
77  * P^{ij,\rm thin} = \frac{S^i S^j}{S^k S_k} E
78  * \f}
79  * with \f$ E \f$, \f$ S^{i}
80  * \f$ the 0th and 1st moments in the inertial frame (see paper for the
81  * computation of \f$P_{ij,thick}\f$ from the other moments).
82  *
83  * The main step of this function is to solve the equation
84  * \f{align}{
85  * \frac{\xi^2 J^2 - H^aH_a}{E^2} = 0
86  * \f}
87  * for \f$\xi\f$, with \f$J\f$ and \f$H^a\f$ being functions
88  * of \f$\xi\f$ and of the input variables (the 0th and 1st moments
89  * in the inertial frame). This is done by separating \f$H^a\f$ as
90  * \f{align}{
91  * H_a = - H_t t_a - H_v v_a - H_f F_a
92  * \f}
93  * (with \f$v^a\f$ the fluid 3-velocity and \f$t^a\f$ the unit normal
94  * to the slice) and then writing each of the
95  * variables \f$J\f$, \f$H_n\f$, \f$H_v\f$, \f$H_f\f$ as
96  * \f{align}{
97  * X = X_0 + d_{\rm thin} X_{\rm thin} + d_{\rm thick} X_{\rm thick}
98  * \f}
99  * so that evaluating
100  * \f{align}{
101  * \frac{\xi^2 J^2 - H^aH_a}{E^2} = 0
102  * \f}
103  * for a given \f$\xi\f$ only requires recomputing \f$d_{\rm thin,thick}\f$
104  * and their derivatives with respect to \f$\xi\f$.
105  * We perform the root-finding using a Newton-Raphson algorithm, with the
106  * accuracy set by the variable root_find_number_of_digits (6 significant digits
107  * at the moment).
108  *
109  * The function returns the closure factors \f$\xi\f$ (to be used as initial
110  * guess for this function at the next step), the pressure tensor \f$P_{ij}\f$,
111  * and the neutrino moments in the frame comoving with the fluid.
112  * The momentum density in the frame comoving with the fluid
113  * is decomposed into its normal component \f$ H^a t_a\f$, and its spatial
114  * components \f$ \gamma_{ia} H^a\f$.
115  */
