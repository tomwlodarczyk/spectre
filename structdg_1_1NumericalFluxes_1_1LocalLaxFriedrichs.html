Detailed Description

template<typename System>
struct dg::NumericalFluxes::LocalLaxFriedrichs< System >

Compute the local Lax-Friedrichs numerical flux.

Let \(U\) and \(F^i(U)\) be the state vector of the system and its corresponding volume flux, respectively. Let \(n_i\) be the unit normal to the interface. Defining \(F_n(U) := n_i F^i(U)\), and denoting the corresponding projections of the numerical fluxes as \({F_n}^*(U)\), the local Lax-Friedrichs flux for each variable is

\begin{align*} {F_n}^*(U) = \frac{F_n(U_\text{int}) + F_n(U_\text{ext})}{2} + \frac{\alpha}{2}\left( U_\text{int} - U_\text{ext}\right), \end{align*}

where "int" and "ext" stand for interior and exterior, respectively, and \(\alpha\) is the maximum eigenvalue of either \(|\Lambda(U_\text{int}; n_\text{int})|\) or \(|\Lambda(U_\text{ext}; n_\text{ext})|\), where \(\Lambda(U; n)\) is the diagonal matrix whose entries are the characteristic speeds of the system.
