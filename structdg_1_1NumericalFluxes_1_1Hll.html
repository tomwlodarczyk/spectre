Detailed Description

template<typename System>
struct dg::NumericalFluxes::Hll< System >

Compute the HLL numerical flux.

Let \(U\) and \(F^i(U)\) be the state vector of the system and its corresponding volume flux, respectively. Let \(n_i\) be the unit normal to the interface. Defining \(F_n(U) := n_i F^i(U)\), and denoting the corresponding projections of the numerical fluxes as \({F_n}^*(U)\), the HLL flux [1] for each variable is

\begin{align*} {F_n}^*(U) = \frac{c_\text{max} F_n(U_\text{int}) - c_\text{min} F_n(U_\text{ext})}{c_\text{max} - c_\text{min}} - \frac{c_\text{min}c_\text{max}}{c_\text{max} - c_\text{min}} \left(U_\text{int} - U_\text{ext}\right) \end{align*}

where "int" and "ext" stand for interior and exterior, respectively. \(c_\text{min}\) and \(c_\text{max}\) are estimates on the minimum and maximum signal velocities bounding the interior-moving and exterior-moving wavespeeds that arise when solving the Riemann problem. Here we use the simple estimates [2]

\begin{align*} c_\text{min} &= \text{min}\left( \lambda_1(U_\text{int}; n_\text{int}), \lambda_1(U_\text{ext}; n_\text{ext}), 0\right),\\ c_\text{max} &= \text{max}\left( \lambda_N(U_\text{int}; n_\text{int}), \lambda_N(U_\text{ext}; n_\text{ext}), 0\right), \end{align*}

where \(\lambda_1(U; n) \leq \lambda_2(U; n) \leq ... \leq \lambda_N(U; n)\) are the (ordered) characteristic speeds of the system. Note that the definitions above ensure that \(c_\text{min} \leq 0\) and \(c_\text{max} \geq 0\). Also, for either \(c_\text{min} = 0\) or \(c_\text{max} = 0\) (i.e. all characteristics move in the same direction) the HLL flux reduces to pure upwinding.

[1] A. Harten, P. D. Lax, B. van Leer, On Upstream Differencing and Godunov-Type Schemes for Hyperbolic Conservation Laws, SIAM Rev. 25 (1983) 35

[2] S. F. Davis, Simplified Second-Order Godunov-Type Methods, SIAM J. Sci. Stat. Comput. 9 (1988) 445
