19 /// Create a 3D Domain consisting of eight rotated Blocks.
20 ///
21 /// \image html eightcubes_rotated_exploded.png
22 ///
23 /// The orientations of the blocks are described in two different ways:
24 ///
25 /// - 1 - As orientations of blocks relative to the physical cartesian axes.
26 ///
27 /// - 2 - As rotations relative to the physical cartesian axes written using
28 /// Rubik's cube rotation notation, for the sake of shorter variable names.
29 ///
30 /// For reference, this is the notation used:
31 ///
32 /// - U - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the +z axis (The "up" side)
33 ///
34 /// - R - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the +x axis (The "right" side)
35 ///
36 /// - F - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the -y axis (The "front" side)
37 ///
38 /// For reference, D, L, and B ("down", "left", and "back") are the inverse
39 /// rotation to the aforementioned ones, respectively.
40 /// Note: Whereas Rubik's cube rotations rotate a layer of the 3x3 puzzle
41 /// cube, we are adopting the notation to apply to rotations of the
42 /// cube itself.
43 ///
44 /// - The -x, -y, -z block has the aligned orientation, that is,
45 /// xi is aligned with x, eta is aligned with y, and zeta with z.
46 ///
47 /// - The +x, -y, -z block has the orientation (zeta, eta, -xi).
48 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F.
49 ///
50 /// - The -x, +y, -z block has the orientation (xi, zeta, -eta).
51 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation R.
52 ///
53 /// - The +x, +y, -z block has the orientation (zeta, -xi, -eta).
54 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F
55 /// followed by the rotation R.
56 ///
57 /// - The -x, -y, +z block has the orientation (eta, -xi, zeta).
58 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation U.
59 ///
60 /// - The +x, -y, +z block has the orientation (eta, -zeta, -xi).
61 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F
62 /// followed by the rotation U.
63 ///
64 /// - The -x, +y, +z block has the orientation (zeta, -xi, -eta).
65 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation R
66 /// followed by the rotation U (equivalently, F followed by R).
67 ///
68 /// - The +x, +y, +z block also has the aligned orientation
69 /// (xi, eta, zeta), relative to the edifice. It is not aligned
70 /// relative to its neighbors.
71 ///
72 /// This DomainCreator is useful for testing code that deals with
73 /// unaligned blocks.
